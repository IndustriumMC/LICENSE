# Contributing to Industrium

Thank you for your interest in contributing to the Industrium project.  
Please read this document carefully before submitting any work.

---

## 1. Who Can Contribute

| Repository Type | Who May Contribute |
|---|---|
| **Private repositories** | Altitude Interactive staff and Authorised Personnel only. External contributions are not accepted. |
| **Public repositories** | Anyone, subject to the terms below. |

If you are unsure whether a repository is private or public, check its root `LICENSE` file or contact a core maintainer.

---

## 2. Licensing of Contributions

### Public repositories

By submitting a contribution (pull request, patch, issue attachment, etc.) to any **public** Industrium repository you agree that:

1. Your contribution is made under the [MIT License](./LICENSE-PUBLIC) that governs the repository.
2. You have the legal right to make the contribution (i.e. you are the author, or you have permission from the author).
3. You grant Altitude Interactive a perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free licence to use, reproduce, modify, and distribute your contribution as part of the project.

### Private repositories

Access to private repositories is by explicit invitation only. Contributions by Authorised Personnel are covered by the [Proprietary License](./LICENSE-PRIVATE) and any separate confidentiality or employment agreement in force.

---

## 3. Code of Conduct

All contributors are expected to:

- Be respectful and professional in all communications.
- Not disclose confidential information from private repositories.
- Follow any additional guidelines specified in the individual repository's `CONTRIBUTING.md` (if present).

---

## 4. How to Submit a Contribution (Public Repositories)

1. **Fork** the repository and create a descriptive branch (e.g. `feature/add-player-tracking`).
2. Make your changes with clear, focused commits.
3. Ensure your changes do not break existing tests or functionality.
4. Open a **pull request** against the `main` branch and fill in the PR template.
5. A core maintainer will review your PR. Please be patient — reviews may take several days.
